## Hiring Process

TCS's hiring process differs slightly for campus freshers versus lateral/experienced hires, but
both funnel through a similar shape: an online assessment, one or two interview rounds, and an HR
round.

## For freshers (TCS NQT)

1. **TCS NQT (National Qualifier Test)** — a proctored online test with sections on quantitative
   aptitude, reasoning, verbal ability, and a programming/coding section (usually 1-2 coding
   problems in a language of choice, plus MCQs on programming fundamentals). This is the biggest
   filter — most candidates are eliminated here.
2. **Technical Interview** — covers the candidate's core CS fundamentals (OOP, DBMS, OS,
   networking basics), the primary language on their resume, and any academic/personal projects
   listed. Interviewers probe for clarity of thought over depth — TCS trains hires post-onboarding,
   so they're assessing trainability more than expert-level knowledge.
3. **HR Interview** — assesses communication, willingness to relocate across TCS's pan-India (and
   sometimes global) delivery centers, career motivation, and basic culture fit. This round is
   rarely a hard filter for candidates who clear the technical round.

## For lateral/experienced hires

1. **Resume screening** against the specific role's tech-stack requirements.
2. **1-2 Technical Interviews** — deeper dive into the specific stack (e.g. Java/Spring Boot, SAP
   module, cloud platform) and past project ownership.
3. **HR/Managerial round** — compensation discussion, project/domain fitment, and notice period.

## Timeline

Campus hiring (NQT to offer) typically takes 4-8 weeks including result declaration. Lateral
hiring can move faster, sometimes 2-3 weeks end-to-end for urgent requirements.

## What's assessed at each stage

The NQT filters for aptitude and basic coding ability; the technical round filters for CS
fundamentals and communication clarity; the HR round filters for logistics fit (location,
notice period) and cultural alignment rather than technical competence.
