## How TCS Probes Your Resume

TCS interviewers typically pick one or two projects from your resume and ask you to explain them
end-to-end in simple terms — what problem it solved, what your specific role was, and what
technology choices you made. They rarely go deep into obscure internals; instead they check
whether you genuinely understand what you built (versus having memorized a description) and
whether you can communicate it clearly to someone unfamiliar with the project.

Common follow-up angles:
- "Why did you choose this technology/library over an alternative?"
- "What was the most challenging part of this project, and how did you solve it?"
- "If you had more time, what would you improve?"
- "Did you work on this alone or in a team, and what was your specific contribution?"

Candidates listing modern stacks (React, FastAPI, cloud platforms, AI/ML libraries) should expect
at least one question checking genuine understanding versus surface familiarity — e.g. being asked
to explain a core concept behind the tool, not just that it was used.
