## HR Questions

### Tell me about yourself.
A good answer walks through education, 1-2 relevant projects or internships, and closes with why
you're interested in an IT services career — keep it under 90 seconds and avoid just repeating the
resume line by line.

### Why do you want to join TCS?
Cover TCS's scale and breadth of client exposure, the structured training programs (iON, Digital
Ignite), and the chance to work across multiple domains/technologies over a career — avoid
generic "great company, great culture" filler.

### Are you willing to relocate to any TCS location in India?
TCS explicitly screens for this. A confident "yes" (if true) removes a major point of friction;
if there are real constraints, state them honestly and explain the boundaries clearly rather than
being vague.

### What do you know about TCS's business?
Mention it's part of the Tata Group, is one of the largest IT services companies globally, and
serves clients across banking, retail, healthcare, and manufacturing through digital engineering,
cloud, and enterprise application services.

### Describe a challenge you faced in a project and how you handled it.
Use a specific, real example (academic project, internship, or personal project) — structure as
situation, action taken, and outcome/learning, not just "it was hard and I fixed it."

### What are your strengths and weaknesses?
Pick a strength you can back with a concrete example. For weaknesses, choose something real but
non-disqualifying, and describe a concrete step you're taking to improve it.

### How do you handle working in a team with conflicting opinions?
TCS values collaborative, process-driven delivery — emphasize listening, finding common ground,
and escalating constructively rather than "just doing it my way."

### Where do you see yourself in 5 years?
Frame around growing technical depth, taking on more ownership/leadership within TCS, rather than
naming a competitor or an unrelated career path.

### Why should we hire you?
Connect 2-3 specific skills or experiences directly to what TCS hires for (adaptability, coding
fundamentals, communication) rather than generic self-praise.

### Are you comfortable with rotational shifts or on-call support work?
Some TCS projects (especially BFSI/support accounts) require shift work — answer honestly, but
show flexibility if you can offer it.

### Do you have any questions for us?
Always have 1-2 genuine questions ready — e.g. about the specific project/account you'd be
placed on, or the training path for your role — asking nothing reads as disengaged.

### How do you stay updated with new technology?
Mention specific habits: following release notes, doing small side projects, online courses
(Coursera/Udemy), or contributing to personal GitHub projects.
