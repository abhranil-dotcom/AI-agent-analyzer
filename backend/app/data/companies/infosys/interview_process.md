## Hiring Process

## For freshers

1. **Online Assessment** — sections on logical reasoning, quantitative aptitude, verbal ability,
   and a pseudocode/coding section. Higher-scoring candidates may be tracked for the
   **Power Programmer** stream, which has a tougher coding round (2-3 DSA problems) and leads to
   product-engineering-style roles rather than generalist service roles.
2. **Technical Interview** — covers programming fundamentals, the primary language on the
   resume, DBMS/OOP basics, and academic or personal projects. For Power Programmer candidates,
   this round is noticeably harder and includes live coding.
3. **HR Interview** — covers relocation flexibility (Infosys campuses span multiple Indian
   cities), communication skills, and career motivation.

## For lateral/experienced hires

1. **Resume screening** matched against the specific role's tech stack.
2. **1-2 Technical Interviews** — deep dive into the candidate's primary stack, system design for
   more senior roles, and past project ownership.
3. **HR/Managerial round** — compensation, project fitment, and notice period discussion.

## InfyTQ

Many candidates (especially from Infosys's campus partner colleges) take InfyTQ courses and
certifications before applying — strong InfyTQ scores can sometimes substitute for parts of the
initial assessment or strengthen a resume during screening.

## Timeline

Campus hiring typically takes 4-6 weeks from assessment to offer, with an additional gap before
the multi-month Mysore training program begins. Lateral hiring is usually faster, around 2-4
weeks.

## What's assessed at each stage

The online assessment filters for aptitude and coding literacy (with Power Programmer as a
harder track for stronger coders); the technical round filters for CS fundamentals and
communication; the HR round filters for logistics and cultural fit.
