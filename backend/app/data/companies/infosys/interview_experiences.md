## Candidate Experiences

### Candidate A — Systems Engineer (Campus, general track)
"The online assessment had aptitude sections plus a pseudocode section where you predict output
rather than write full code. I didn't score high enough for Power Programmer, so I went through
the general technical interview — questions on OOP concepts using my final-year project, a couple
of SQL join questions, and a basic linked-list reversal on a whiteboard. The interviewer was
patient and gave hints when I got stuck. HR asked about relocation to Mysore for training, which I
was already expecting."

### Candidate B — Power Programmer (Campus)
"I scored well on the coding section, so I got tracked into Power Programmer. That interview was
much harder than what my friends in the general track described — two DSA problems (a graph BFS
question and a dynamic programming one on knapsack), plus a deep dive into time complexity for
each. They also asked me to explain a personal GitHub project in detail. It felt closer to a
product-company interview than a typical service-company one."

### Candidate C — Lateral hire, Java/Spring Boot developer
"As a lateral candidate with 3 years of experience, there was no aptitude test — just two
technical rounds. The first covered Spring Boot, REST API design, and a live debugging exercise
in a codebase they shared beforehand. The second was more architecture-focused: how would you
design a caching layer, how do you handle database migrations safely. HR discussed comp bands and
notice period. Total process took about 3 weeks."

### Candidate D — Cloud Engineer (Lateral, AWS/Azure)
"Technical round covered cloud fundamentals broadly — IaaS vs PaaS vs SaaS, shared responsibility
model, and a scenario question about migrating an on-prem app to Azure. They also asked basic
scripting questions in Python. The interviewer was clearly evaluating breadth of cloud knowledge
rather than deep specialization in one provider. HR round was standard — logistics and
compensation."
