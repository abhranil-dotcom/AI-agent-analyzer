## How Capgemini Probes Your Resume

Capgemini interviewers tend to use resume projects as a way to assess both technical substance and
communication clarity simultaneously — you're often asked to explain a project as if to a client
who isn't technical, then asked follow-up technical questions once the interviewer is satisfied
you can communicate clearly. Given Capgemini's international client base, expect questions framed
around collaboration and cross-team communication around your projects, not just the tech itself.

Common follow-up angles:
- "How would you explain this project to a non-technical client?"
- "What technology choices did you make, and why those specifically?"
- "Did you collaborate with anyone outside your immediate team on this?"
- "What was the most difficult trade-off you had to make?"

For projects involving cloud platforms, APIs, or modern AI tooling, expect a question checking
genuine understanding of the underlying concept, framed practically (e.g. "what problem does this
actually solve for the user?").
