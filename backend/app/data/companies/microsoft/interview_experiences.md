## Candidate Experiences

### Candidate A — SDE (New grad, full loop)
"Phone screen was a linked-list reversal question plus some light behavioral chat. Onsite (done
virtually over Teams) had 4 rounds: two coding rounds (lowest common ancestor, and K closest
points), one growth-mindset behavioral round, and a final 'As Appropriate' round with a senior
engineer who mostly asked me to walk back through my whole day and clarify a couple of answers
from earlier rounds. It felt less adversarial than I expected — more like a technical
conversation than an interrogation."

### Candidate B — SDE II (Lateral, Azure team)
"Coding rounds were solid Medium-Hard difficulty (a distinct-islands variant, and a basic
calculator/parser problem). The system design round was Azure-flavored — they explicitly framed
the question around designing something like Blob Storage and wanted me to reference real
consistency/durability trade-offs. The growth-mindset round asked directly about a time I was
wrong about a technical decision — being honest and specific about what I learned clearly landed
better than a vague answer."

### Candidate C — Cloud Solution Architect (Lateral)
"Less pure-algorithm-heavy than the SDE track — more scenario- and design-oriented, with one
round entirely about migrating a hypothetical enterprise workload to Azure and discussing shared
responsibility model implications. Still had one coding round (SQL window functions for a
reporting scenario). The 'As Appropriate' round felt like a genuine sanity check rather than a
gatekeeping formality."

### Candidate D — SDE (New grad, resume deep-dive)
"One interviewer spent almost half the round on my resume project — a LangChain and Azure OpenAI
based resume analyzer — asking about my retrieval pipeline design and why I chose Azure OpenAI
specifically. They seemed genuinely curious rather than just checking a box, which matched what
I'd read about Microsoft's growth-mindset culture beforehand."
