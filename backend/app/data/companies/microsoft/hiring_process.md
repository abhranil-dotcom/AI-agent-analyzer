## Hiring Process

1. **Resume screening & recruiter call** — initial fit discussion and role matching.
2. **Phone/Video Screen** — one round with a Medium coding problem and light behavioral
   questions, often conducted via Microsoft Teams with a shared coding environment.
3. **Onsite/Virtual Loop (4-5 rounds)**:
   - **2-3 Coding rounds** — Medium-to-Hard DSA problems, evaluated on correctness, code quality,
     and complexity analysis.
   - **System Design round** (mainly for SDE II and above) — designing a scalable service with
     attention to Azure-style cloud trade-offs (many interviewers reference real Azure services
     as examples).
   - **Growth Mindset / Behavioral round** — explicitly probes for curiosity, how you respond to
     being wrong or challenged, and collaborative (not competitive) framing of past work.
   - **"As Appropriate" (AA) round** — a senior engineer or manager outside the immediate team
     reviews the whole loop's signal and makes the final call, similar in spirit to a Bar Raiser
     but framed around holistic fit rather than a single Leadership Principle checklist.
4. **Debrief** — interviewers discuss feedback together, with the AA interviewer's read carrying
   significant weight in edge cases.

## Timeline

Typically 3-6 weeks from phone screen to offer, with some variance by team and role level.

## What's assessed at each stage

The phone screen filters for baseline coding ability and communication; onsite coding rounds
assess DSA depth and code quality; system design (for senior roles) assesses cloud-scale
architecture thinking; the growth-mindset round assesses genuine curiosity and collaborative
instincts; the AA round exists specifically to sanity-check the overall signal before an offer is
extended.
