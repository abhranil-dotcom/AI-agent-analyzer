## How Microsoft Probes Your Resume

Microsoft interviewers often spend meaningful time on one resume project, framed as a genuine,
collaborative technical conversation rather than an interrogation. They're looking for clear
reasoning about trade-offs and genuine curiosity about alternative approaches, consistent with the
company's growth-mindset culture.

Common follow-up angles:
- "What was the hardest technical decision in this project, and why did you make it that way?"
- "If you rebuilt this today, what would you do differently?"
- "How would this design change if you needed to scale it significantly?"
- "What's a limitation of your current approach, and how would you address it?"

For projects using modern AI tooling or cloud platforms (LangChain, Azure OpenAI, FastAPI),
expect a genuinely curious follow-up on why that specific choice was made over alternatives, not
just confirmation that it was used.
