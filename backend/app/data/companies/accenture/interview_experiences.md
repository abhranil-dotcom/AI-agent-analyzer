## Candidate Experiences

### Candidate A — Associate Software Engineer (Campus)
"The assessment had cognitive/aptitude sections plus a coding round. The technical interview
was noticeably more conversational than I expected — instead of just asking 'what is
polymorphism,' the interviewer asked me to explain a decision I made in my project and what
trade-offs I considered. They also asked a values-fit question directly: 'what does client value
creation mean to you?' HR round covered relocation and career motivation, plus a values-alignment
discussion."

### Candidate B — Cloud Engineer (Lateral, 2 years experience)
"Two technical rounds. First was hands-on cloud knowledge — serverless trade-offs, IaC benefits,
and a scenario about reducing costs for an over-provisioned environment. Second round was a system
design discussion: design a rate limiter for an API. The interviewer kept pushing on 'why' for
every design choice, which felt very consulting-flavored compared to a pure technical grilling.
HR discussed comp and account placement."

### Candidate C — Data Engineer (Lateral)
"Technical round covered data warehouse vs data lake trade-offs, a SQL window-function question
(running totals), and a discussion of a past data pipeline project — they wanted to know not just
what I built but why I chose that architecture given the client's constraints. Very
business-context-aware questioning throughout."

### Candidate D — Security Analyst (Campus, security track)
"My track had a slightly different assessment with more security-oriented aptitude questions.
Technical interview covered authentication vs authorization, a man-in-the-middle attack scenario,
and general networking basics. The interviewer was patient with gaps in specialized knowledge and
seemed more focused on reasoning ability and genuine interest in security as a field."
