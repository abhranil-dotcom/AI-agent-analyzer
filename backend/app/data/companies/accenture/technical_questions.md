## Core CS Fundamentals

### Explain polymorphism and why it's useful in large systems.
Expect a follow-up connecting the concept to maintainability/extensibility in real projects, not
just the textbook definition.

### What is the difference between composition and inheritance?
Cover "has-a" vs "is-a" relationships and when composition is preferred for flexibility.

### Explain the CAP theorem at a high level.
Cover Consistency, Availability, Partition tolerance, and why you can only fully guarantee two of
the three in a distributed system.

### What is the difference between vertical and horizontal scaling, and when would you choose each?
Expect a business-impact-aware answer (cost, downtime, complexity), not just a definition.

## Programming / Backend

### Explain RESTful API design principles.
Cover statelessness, resource-based URLs, proper HTTP verb usage, and versioning.

### What is dependency injection, and why does it matter for testability?
Cover loose coupling and how it enables mocking dependencies in unit tests.

### Explain the difference between SQL and NoSQL databases, and when you'd choose each.
Cover schema flexibility, consistency guarantees, and typical use cases (e.g. transactional data
vs high-write unstructured data).

### What is a microservices architecture's biggest trade-off compared to a monolith?
Expect discussion of operational complexity (deployment, monitoring, inter-service
communication) versus independent scalability/deployability.

## Cloud & Data

### Explain the difference between a data warehouse and a data lake.
Cover structured/curated vs raw/flexible storage and typical use cases for each.

### What is serverless computing, and what are its trade-offs?
Cover reduced operational overhead vs cold-start latency and vendor lock-in considerations.

### Explain the concept of Infrastructure as Code and its business benefit.
Cover repeatability, auditability, and reduced manual configuration drift — tie to
cost/reliability impact.

## Security (for security-track candidates)

### What is the difference between authentication and authorization?
Cover "who you are" vs "what you're allowed to do" with a concrete example.

### Explain what a man-in-the-middle attack is and one mitigation.
Cover interception of communication and mitigations like TLS/certificate pinning.

## SAP (for SAP-track candidates)

### What is the role of a functional consultant in an SAP implementation project?
Cover translating business requirements into system configuration, distinct from ABAP
development.
