## Preparation Tips

- Practice coding on a shared doc or whiteboard-style environment (no autocomplete) — Google
  explicitly evaluates how you think out loud while solving, not just the final answer.
- Focus preparation on core CS fundamentals and algorithms rather than any specific language or
  framework — Google interviews are deliberately stack-agnostic.
- Practice precise complexity analysis for every solution; clean, readable code under time
  pressure is explicitly scored.
- For system design rounds, practice discussing consistency/availability trade-offs (CAP theorem)
  with concrete examples.
- Prepare genuine, specific "Googleyness" answers — collaboration, comfort with ambiguity, and
  intellectual humility — generic answers are easy to spot.
- Be ready for a resume deep-dive round; know your projects well enough to defend specific design
  decisions in detail.
