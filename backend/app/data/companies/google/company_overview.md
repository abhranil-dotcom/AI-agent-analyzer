## About Google

Google is a global technology leader spanning search, advertising, cloud computing (Google
Cloud Platform), consumer devices (Pixel, Android), and productivity software (Workspace).
Engineering culture places an unusually strong emphasis on computer science fundamentals,
large-scale distributed systems, and rigorous, structured interviewing designed to be consistent
across every team and geography.

## Business lines relevant to tech hiring

- **Search & Ads**: the core of Google's business — large-scale information retrieval, ranking,
  and advertising systems.
- **Google Cloud Platform (GCP)**: enterprise cloud infrastructure and platform services,
  competing directly with AWS and Azure.
- **Android & Devices**: mobile OS engineering and Pixel hardware/software integration.
- **YouTube & Workspace**: large-scale media infrastructure and productivity software
  respectively.
- **AI/ML (Google DeepMind, Google Research)**: fundamental and applied AI research feeding into
  products across the company.

## Culture and hiring style

Google's interview process is famously structured and fundamentals-heavy: coding rounds focus on
algorithms, data structures, and complexity analysis rather than any specific framework or
language, on the theory that strong fundamentals transfer across any tech stack Google uses
internally. A distinctive **"Googleyness and Leadership"** round assesses culture fit, comfort
with ambiguity, and collaborative problem-solving. Hiring decisions are made by an independent
**hiring committee** that reviews written feedback from all interviewers, deliberately separating
the decision from the hiring manager alone to reduce bias and maintain a consistent bar globally.

## Typical roles hired for

Software Engineer (L3-L6+), Site Reliability Engineer (SRE), Product Manager (non-engineering but
often paired in the same broader hiring process), and Research Scientist/Engineer roles feeding
into Google Research and DeepMind.
