## Hiring Process

1. **Resume screening & recruiter phone call** — a recruiter discusses background, interests, and
   role fit before scheduling technical rounds.
2. **Phone Screen(s) (1-2 rounds)** — live coding on a shared doc/IDE, typically 1-2 Medium
   algorithm problems, assessed on correctness, clean code, and clear verbal reasoning while
   coding (Google explicitly evaluates how you think out loud, not just the final answer).
3. **Onsite Loop (4-5 rounds)**, generally including:
   - **2-3 Coding/Algorithms rounds** — Medium-to-Hard DSA problems with heavy emphasis on
     complexity analysis and edge-case handling.
   - **System Design round** (for more senior levels) — designing a large-scale distributed
     system with attention to consistency, availability, and fault tolerance trade-offs.
   - **Googleyness & Leadership round** — behavioral, focused on collaboration, comfort with
     ambiguity, and how you've navigated conflict or failure.
4. **Hiring Committee Review** — all interviewer feedback (written, structured, and detailed) is
   reviewed by a committee that wasn't in the interview room, specifically to keep the bar
   consistent across teams and remove any single interviewer's bias from the final call.
5. **Team Matching** — for many roles, an offer is extended before a specific team is finalized,
   followed by a team-matching process.

## Timeline

Typically 4-8 weeks from first phone screen to offer, sometimes longer due to hiring committee
scheduling and team-matching.

## What's assessed at each stage

Phone screens filter for baseline algorithmic ability and communication while problem-solving;
onsite coding rounds assess DSA depth, code quality, and complexity analysis rigor; system design
(for senior levels) assesses distributed-systems thinking; Googleyness assesses collaboration and
culture fit; the hiring committee stage exists specifically to protect a consistent bar
independent of any single interviewer's judgment.
