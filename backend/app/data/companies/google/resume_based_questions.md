## How Google Probes Your Resume

Google interviewers frequently dedicate an entire round to a deep dive on one resume project,
treating it as a genuine technical conversation rather than a formality. Expect sustained
follow-ups on specific design decisions, scalability, and trade-offs — the interviewer is
assessing depth of understanding and clarity of technical communication simultaneously.

Common follow-up angles:
- "Why this architecture/approach instead of the alternatives?"
- "How would you scale this to 10x or 100x the current usage?"
- "What's the biggest weakness or limitation of your current design?"
- "Walk me through a specific technical challenge you hit and how you debugged it."

For projects involving retrieval/AI systems, cloud infrastructure, or custom data structures,
expect the interviewer to probe the actual mechanics (e.g. "why a vector database instead of just
including everything in the prompt?") rather than accepting tool names at face value.
