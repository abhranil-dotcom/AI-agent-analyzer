## Candidate Experiences

### Candidate A — SDE I (New grad, full onsite loop)
"The OA had two Medium LeetCode-style problems (a sliding-window string problem and a graph BFS
problem) plus a work-style assessment full of Leadership Principle scenarios. The onsite loop was
5 rounds in one day: two coding rounds (LRU cache design, and merge K sorted lists), one system
design round even though I was a new grad (they asked me to design a simplified URL shortener),
and two behavioral-heavy rounds. Every single round, including the coding ones, ended with 'tell
me about a time when...' questions. The Bar Raiser round was the toughest — very deep follow-up
questions on a failure story I told, checking for genuine ownership and learning."

### Candidate B — SDE II (Lateral, AWS team)
"Phone screen was a Medium graph problem (number of islands) plus STAR-format behavioral
questions. Onsite had a harder coding round (Dijkstra's shortest path), a full system design round
(design a notification system at scale), and two Leadership-Principle-focused rounds. They pushed
hard on 'Dive Deep' — for every design choice I made, they asked 'why not X instead?' repeatedly.
The Bar Raiser explicitly told me afterward that consistency of signal across all interviewers
mattered more than any single strong round."

### Candidate C — Cloud Support Engineer (Lateral, AWS)
"Less algorithm-heavy than the SDE track — more scenario-based troubleshooting questions (e.g.
'a customer's EC2 instance is unreachable, walk me through your diagnosis'), plus one moderate
coding round. Behavioral questions were still very much Leadership-Principles-driven, especially
'Customer Obsession' and 'Ownership.' The process felt just as rigorous as the SDE loop despite
being a different track."

### Candidate D — SDE I (New grad, referencing a personal AI project)
"They spent a surprising amount of time on my resume project — a LangChain-based resume analyzer
using Azure OpenAI. The interviewer asked why I chose Azure OpenAI over the direct OpenAI API, how
my agent's retrieval pipeline worked, and what I'd do differently at scale. It reinforced that
Amazon interviewers really do 'Dive Deep' into anything on your resume, not just prepared
algorithm questions."
