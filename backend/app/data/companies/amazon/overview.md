## About Amazon

Amazon is a global technology and e-commerce leader, with Amazon Web Services (AWS) as its
dominant cloud computing arm alongside its retail, logistics, devices, and entertainment
businesses. Technology roles span everything from consumer-facing e-commerce systems to
large-scale distributed cloud infrastructure powering AWS itself.

## Business lines relevant to tech hiring

- **AWS**: cloud infrastructure, platform services, and enterprise cloud solutions — one of the
  largest and most technically demanding engineering organizations at Amazon.
- **Retail & e-commerce technology**: search, recommendations, fulfillment/logistics systems, and
  customer-facing web/mobile experiences at massive scale.
- **Devices & entertainment**: Alexa, Kindle, Prime Video engineering.
- **Advertising**: Amazon Ads' demand-side and supply-side ad-tech platforms.

## Culture and hiring style

Amazon's hiring and internal culture are built around its **16 Leadership Principles** (e.g.
Customer Obsession, Ownership, Bias for Action, Dive Deep, Deliver Results) — every interview,
including technical rounds, includes behavioral questions explicitly mapped to these principles,
and answers are expected in the **STAR format** (Situation, Task, Action, Result). A dedicated
**Bar Raiser** — an interviewer from outside the hiring team trained to protect hiring quality
bar — sits in on the loop and has veto power over the hire decision. Technical bar is high, with a
strong emphasis on data structures, algorithms, and the ability to design systems that scale.

## Typical roles hired for

Software Development Engineer (SDE I/II/III), Applied Scientist, Cloud Support Engineer, Solutions
Architect (AWS-facing), and Data Engineer — across a very wide range of seniority from new-grad to
principal-level.
