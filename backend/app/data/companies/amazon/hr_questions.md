## HR / Behavioral Questions (Leadership Principles-based)

Amazon's behavioral questions are explicitly mapped to its Leadership Principles and expect
answers in **STAR format** (Situation, Task, Action, Result) with a real, specific example — not
a hypothetical.

### Tell me about a time you disagreed with your manager or team and how you handled it.
Maps to "Have Backbone; Disagree and Commit" — show you can respectfully push back with data, but
also fully commit once a decision is made.

### Describe a time you went above and beyond what was required.
Maps to "Ownership" — use a concrete example where you took responsibility beyond your explicit
task scope.

### Tell me about a time you had to make a decision with incomplete information.
Maps to "Bias for Action" and "Dive Deep" — show a structured approach to decision-making under
uncertainty, not recklessness.

### Describe a time you failed at something and what you learned.
Maps to "Learn and Be Curious" — be genuinely honest about the failure, not a disguised success
story, and clearly articulate the lesson.

### Tell me about a time you had to deal with a difficult stakeholder or customer.
Maps to "Customer Obsession" — show you prioritized the customer/user's actual needs.

### Describe the most complex technical problem you've solved.
Maps to "Dive Deep" — be ready to go into real technical depth if pressed with follow-up
questions.

### Tell me about a time you simplified something that was overly complex.
Maps to "Invent and Simplify."

### Why do you want to work at Amazon?
Connect genuinely to specific Leadership Principles or a specific team/product you're excited
about — avoid generic "innovative company" answers.

### Tell me about a time you had to deliver results under a tight deadline.
Maps to "Deliver Results" — quantify the outcome where possible.

### Describe a time you took a calculated risk.
Maps to "Bias for Action" — show reasoned risk-taking, not recklessness.

### How do you prioritize when you have multiple competing tasks?
Maps to "Deliver Results" and "Ownership" — describe a real prioritization framework you've used.

### Do you have any questions for us?
Ask something specific about the team's ownership scope or how they apply a particular Leadership
Principle day-to-day — generic questions read poorly here.
