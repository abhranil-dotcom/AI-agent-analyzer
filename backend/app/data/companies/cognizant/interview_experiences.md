## Candidate Experiences

### Candidate A — Programmer Analyst Trainee (GenC, Campus)
"The assessment was straightforward — aptitude plus two coding questions (array min/max and a
string rotation check). I didn't get tracked into GenC Pro, so my technical interview was fairly
standard: OOP basics, a SQL query on employee data, and questions about my final-year project. The
interviewer asked if I'd be comfortable working on healthcare-related projects since that's a big
part of Cognizant's business — I hadn't thought about it before but said I was open to it, which
seemed to matter to them."

### Candidate B — Programmer Analyst (GenC Pro, Campus)
"I scored well on the coding round, so I got moved into GenC Pro. That interview was noticeably
tougher — a linked-list-reversal-in-groups problem and a HashMap-based pair-sum question, both
with live coding. They also asked more pointed questions about time complexity than I expected
for a fresher interview. Felt closer to a product-company bar than typical service-company
hiring."

### Candidate C — Full Stack Developer (Lateral, BFSI account)
"Since I was applying for a BFSI-focused account, the technical round mixed standard Java/Spring
Boot questions with a scenario about transaction atomicity — why you can't have a partial money
transfer. It was clear they wanted someone who understood why correctness matters more in
financial systems, not just someone who could code. Second round was more general system design.
HR discussed comp and specific account details."

### Candidate D — Data Engineer (Lateral, Healthcare vertical)
"Interesting mix of technical and domain questions — standard SQL/data pipeline questions, plus a
conceptual question about HIPAA and why healthcare data systems need strict access controls. I
didn't have deep healthcare-industry experience, but showing genuine understanding of why privacy
matters in that domain went over well. The interviewer explicitly said domain knowledge could be
built on the job if the technical foundation was solid."
