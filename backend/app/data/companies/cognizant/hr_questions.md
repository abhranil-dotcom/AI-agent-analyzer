## HR Questions

### Tell me about yourself.
Keep it structured: education, key project/internship, and a closing line tying your interests to
Cognizant's industry-focused work (healthcare, BFSI, etc. if relevant to you).

### Why do you want to join Cognizant?
Reference its strong healthcare and BFSI domain expertise, the GenC training pipeline, and the
chance to work on industry-specific, high-impact systems rather than purely generic IT work.

### Are you comfortable working in a regulated industry like healthcare or banking?
Cognizant's flagship accounts are often compliance-heavy — express genuine comfort with process
discipline and, if relevant, mention any exposure to regulated environments.

### Are you willing to relocate across Cognizant's delivery centers?
Answer honestly and confidently if flexible — this is a common filter question.

### Describe a time you had to follow a strict process or set of guidelines.
Directly relevant given Cognizant's regulated-industry focus — use a real, specific example.

### What are your strengths and weaknesses?
Ground the strength in evidence; choose a genuine weakness with a concrete improvement plan.

### Describe a challenge you faced in a team project and how you resolved it.
Structure as situation, action, outcome, emphasizing communication and collaboration.

### Where do you see yourself in 5 years?
Frame around growing technical or domain expertise (e.g. healthcare IT, BFSI) within Cognizant.

### Why should we hire you?
Tie specific skills to what Cognizant hires for — adaptability, coding fundamentals, and comfort
with structured, process-driven delivery.

### How do you handle sensitive or confidential data responsibly?
Relevant given healthcare/BFSI data sensitivity — describe an awareness of data privacy even if
from coursework or reading, not just direct experience.

### Do you have any questions for us?
Ask something specific — e.g. which vertical or account you'd likely be placed in, or what GenC
training covers.

### How do you approach learning domain knowledge outside of pure technology (e.g. healthcare or banking concepts)?
Cognizant values domain adaptability — mention a genuine willingness and any relevant habits
(reading, online courses).
